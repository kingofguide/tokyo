## Overview

**Nothing goes to waste**. Damage converts to energy, the card market becomes a tool, and every bad situation becomes an opportunity. 

As an **Intermediate** monster with a **Harvester** and **Collector** playstyle, Alienoid rewards patience and board reading over brute force.

## Playstyle

The evolution cards define this clearly: **Anger Batteries** converts incoming damage directly into energy, **Superior Alien Technology** makes expensive cards affordable, and **Adapting Technology** reshapes the market on demand. 

The more pressure opponents apply, the more fuel Alienoid generates. The core loop is: **absorb hits, convert to energy, buy powerful cards, escalate**.

## Evolution Cards
Alienoid's eight evolution cards reward adaptation and energy management. You start with one in hand and draw more by rolling three or more hearts.

### Superior Alien Technology *(Permanent)*
*Buy KEEP cards at half cost (rounded up). When you do, place a UFO token on the card. At the start of your turn, roll a die for each KEEP card with a UFO token. Discard any card that rolls a CLAW. Maximum 3 UFO-tokened cards at a time.*

Alienoid's most explosive card. Grabbing a high cost card for half price is potentially game-winning, but the discard risk is real. Prioritise cards that give immediate value rather than long-term engine pieces that need time to pay off.

### Mothership Support *(Permanent)*
*Once per turn, spend 1 ENERGY to gain 1 HEART.*

Converts energy directly into survivability. Lets you stay in Tokyo longer than opponents expect and keeps your health buffer stable without relying on heart dice.

### Exotic Arms *(Permanent)*
*Before you roll, place 2 ENERGY on this card. If you roll at least 3 CLAWS, take the energy back and deal 2 extra damage to each monster you wound. If you don't, lose the 2 ENERGY and lose 2 HEART.*

High risk, high reward. Only gamble with this when you have energy to spare. The downside is punishing.

### Funny Looking But Dangerous *(Permanent)*
*If you roll at least three 2s, every other monster loses 1 HEART.*

Passive and consistent once active. Rolling three 2s happens more often than expected and the chip damage accumulates quickly across the table.

### Adapting Technology *(Permanent)*
*Comes with 3 ALIEN tokens. On your turn, remove a token to discard the 3 face-up Power cards and replace them with 3 new ones. Discarded when all tokens are used.*

A controlled market reset. Best saved for when the market is dead or when an opponent is clearly eyeing a card you want to deny them.

### Anger Batteries *(Temporary)*
*Gain 1 ENERGY for each HEART you lost this turn.*

Alienoid's most on-brand card. Converts punishment directly into fuel. Play it after a heavy damage turn to refill your engine entirely.

### Precision Field Support *(Temporary)*
*Draw Power cards until you reveal a KEEP card costing 4 ENERGY or less. Take it for free and discard the rest.*

A free card from the deck. Best used when the face-up market has nothing worth buying.

### Alien Scourge *(Temporary)*
*Gain 2 STAR.*

Hold it until you are 2 VP from winning and close out cleanly.

## Tips & Strategies

### Early Game
- Prioritise lightning bolts over everything else. The entire kit runs on energy.
- Your opening evolution sets the gameplan. **Mothership Support** means lean into sustain. **Superior Alien Technology** means pivot to card buying immediately.

### Late Game
- Hold **Alien Scourge** until you are exactly 2 VP from winning.
- **Exotic Arms** is safer to gamble with once you have a large energy buffer built up.

### Evolution Combos
- **Anger Batteries + Exotic Arms** — take damage, refuel, then bet the energy on a big claw turn.
- **Superior Alien Technology + Precision Field Support** — half-price buys and a free card in the same round.
- **Mothership Support + Funny Looking But Dangerous** — heal passively while dealing chip damage every turn.

## Common Mistakes
- Gambling **Exotic Arms** when low on health or energy, the penalty hits both
- Using all **Adapting Technology** tokens too early before opponents have stockpiled energy
- Playing **Anger Batteries** before taking damage that turn, it needs to be reactive
- Rolling claws when lightning bolts are almost always the better choice
