## Overview
Alienoid is one of the original six monsters from the base game — a hulking extraterrestrial invader known as the "Space Challenger." What makes Alienoid unique is his philosophy: **nothing goes to waste**. He converts damage into energy, turns the card market to his advantage, and punishes opponents just by rolling dice well.
He requires a bit more reading of the board than Gigazaur or King Lizard, making him a solid step up for players ready to think a turn ahead.

## Playstyle
Alienoid is an **Opportunist**. He doesn't bully the board like Gigazaur or sit quietly like King Lizard — he reacts, adapts, and profits from whatever the game throws at him. His evolution cards lean into this identity hard: taking damage generates energy, cards get cheaper, and the market itself becomes a weapon.
His core loop is: **absorb hits → convert to energy → buy powerful cards → escalate**.
This makes him deceptively good in a crowded game where everyone is constantly dealing damage. The more chaos on the table, the more Alienoid thrives.
## Evolution Cards
Alienoid's eight evolution cards reward adaptation and energy management. You start the game with one in hand and draw more by rolling three or more hearts.
### Superior Alien Technology *(Permanent)*
Buy KEEP cards at half cost (rounded up). When you do, place a UFO token on the card. At the start of your turn, roll a die for each KEEP card with a UFO token — discard any card that rolls a CLAW. You can have a maximum of 3 UFO-tokened cards at a time.
This is Alienoid's most explosive card. Grabbing a 6-8 energy card for 3-4 is potentially game-winning — but the discard risk is real. Prioritise cards that give immediate value even if they get discarded, rather than long-term engine pieces that need time to pay off.
### Mothership Support *(Permanent)*
Once per turn, spend 1 ENERGY to gain 1 HEART.
A reliable, low-key sustain tool. It blurs the line between your two most important resources and lets you stay in Tokyo longer than opponents expect. Even spending 2-3 energy to heal per round adds up to a significant survival advantage over a full game.
### Exotic Arms *(Permanent)*
Before you roll, place 2 ENERGY on this card. If you then roll at least 3 CLAWS, take the energy back and deal 2 extra damage to each monster you wound. If you don't roll 3 CLAWS, lose the 2 ENERGY and lose 2 HEART.
High risk, high reward. Best used when you're already rolling aggressively for claws and have energy to spare. Don't gamble with it when you're low on health or energy — the downside (lose 2 energy and 2 health) is punishing.
### Funny Looking But Dangerous *(Permanent)*
If you roll at least three 2s, every other monster loses 1 HEART.
Passive and consistent once active. Rolling three 2s happens more often than players expect, and pinging every opponent for 1 damage per turn adds up fast — especially in a 4-player game where you're hitting 3 monsters at once. Combines well with other number-fishing dice strategies.
### Adapting Technology *(Permanent)*
Comes with 3 ALIEN tokens. On your turn, remove a token to discard the 3 face-up Power cards and replace them with 3 new ones. Discarded when all tokens are used.
A controlled market reset — not infinite, so use it thoughtfully. Best saved for two scenarios: when the market is completely dead and useless, or when an opponent with a lot of energy is clearly eyeing a card you want to deny them.
### Anger Batteries *(Temporary)*
Gain 1 ENERGY for each HEART you lost this turn.
A perfect Alienoid card in spirit — converts punishment into fuel. Best played after a turn where you took heavy damage, ideally while in Tokyo. If you lost 4-5 health in a single turn, this single card can refill your engine entirely.
### Precision Field Support *(Temporary)*
Draw Power cards from the top of the deck until you reveal a KEEP card costing 4 ENERGY or less. Take that card for free and discard the rest.
A free card tutor. The value depends entirely on what's left in the deck, but grabbing any keep card for free is strong. Play it when the face-up market is bad and you want to dig for something useful.
### Alien Scourge *(Temporary)*
Gain 2 STAR.
Simple and effective. Two free victory points at any moment — best saved for when you're 2 VP short of a win, or to surge ahead after a strong turn.
## Tips & Strategies
### Early Game
- Your opening evolution sets your gameplan. **Mothership Support** or **Anger Batteries** means lean into energy and sustain. **Superior Alien Technology** means pivot to card-buying aggression immediately.
- Roll hearts early — getting more evolutions in hand is important, and Alienoid wants options.
- Prioritise lightning bolts. Your kit rewards energy heavily.
### Mid Game
- Once **Mothership Support** is active, consider staying in Tokyo longer than feels comfortable. Your healing converts energy into survival in a way other monsters cannot replicate.
- **Superior Alien Technology** is your most explosive card — time it when a high-cost card is in the market. Target cards that give immediate value in case they get discarded.
- Save **Adapting Technology** tokens for meaningful moments, not just bad markets.
### Late Game
- **Alien Scourge** is a fantastic closer — hold it until you're 2 VP away from winning.
- Once your engine is running, Alienoid can buy VP-generating keep cards faster than almost anyone else.
- **Exotic Arms** becomes safer to gamble with late game when you have more energy buffer to absorb a failed roll.
### Best Evolution Combos
- **Anger Batteries + Exotic Arms** — take damage, refuel with Anger Batteries, then bet the energy on Exotic Arms for a devastating claw turn.
- **Superior Alien Technology + Precision Field Support** — half-price buys and a free card tutor. You can build a full keep card engine in a single round.
- **Mothership Support + Funny Looking But Dangerous** — sustained healing while passively pinging every opponent for chip damage each turn.
### Card Priorities (Base Market)
1. **Regeneration** — stacks with Mothership Support for exceptional healing
2. **Apartment Building** — energy sink that pays out VP fast
3. **Nova Breath** — hits all outside-Tokyo monsters, synergises with Funny Looking But Dangerous chip damage
4. **Complete Destruction** — nuke the board when you're ready to close out
## Common Mistakes
- Gambling **Exotic Arms** when low on health or energy — the penalty hits both, which can spiral badly
- Using all 3 **Adapting Technology** tokens too early before opponents have built up energy stockpiles
- Playing **Anger Batteries** proactively instead of reactively — wait until you've actually taken big damage that turn
- Ignoring **Funny Looking But Dangerous** as a win condition — consistent 1-damage pings to all opponents is stronger than it looks
- Treating Alienoid like a brawler and rolling claws when lightning bolts fuel his engine far better
