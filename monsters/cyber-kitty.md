## Overview
Chaos incarnate. Fragile, unpredictable, and capable of swinging a game completely in a single turn. The evolutions punish opponents in Tokyo, steal resources directly, and culminate in one of the most dramatic comeback mechanics in the game. 

Rated **Advanced** with a **Disruptor** and **Smasher** playstyle, Cyber Kitty rewards players who enjoy high-variance, high-reward gameplay and aren't afraid of risky turns.

## Playstyle

Two things: **rolling ones** and **stealing resources**. Meow Missile and Mouse Hunter both trigger off a single 1 in your final roll, turning a normally wasted die face into a free claw and a free VP simultaneously. Play With Your Food converts strong claw turns into direct resource theft from Tokyo occupants. 

Nine Lives means elimination isn't the end. The core loop is: **roll ones to amplify, steal resources, hit Tokyo hard, trigger Nine Lives, win from nowhere**.

## Evolution Cards
Cyber Kitty's eight evolution cards build a cohesive kit around rolling ones, stealing resources, and surviving situations that would eliminate any other monster.

### Meow Missile *(Permanent)*
*If you roll at least one 1, add a CLAW to your roll.*

A passive damage amplifier. Most players reroll ones away out of habit. With this active, a single 1 in your final roll means a free claw on top of everything else. Stack with Mouse Hunter for turns where keeping ones is the primary goal.

### Mouse Hunter *(Permanent)*
*If you roll at least one 1, gain 1 STAR.*

A free VP every turn you end with a 1 in your roll. Combined with Meow Missile, a single 1 simultaneously gives a free claw and a free VP. Keeping one die showing a 1 becomes a legitimate strategy rather than a concession.

### Feline Motor *(Permanent)*
*During other monsters' movement phases, if Tokyo is empty, you can take control of it instead of the monster whose turn it is.*

Tokyo control that costs nothing on your own turn. If anyone yields and Tokyo empties during their turn, slide in before the active monster gets there. Opponents can't account for it because it triggers on their turn, not yours.

### Play With Your Food *(Permanent)*
*When a monster in Tokyo must lose at least 2 HEART from your CLAWS, you can make them lose 2 HEART fewer and instead steal 1 STAR and 1 ENERGY from them directly.*

Turns every strong claw turn into a choice. Deal full damage, or steal a star and energy from the Tokyo occupant? Against energy-rich monsters the theft is often worth more than the extra damage.

### Cat Nip *(Temporary)*
*Play at the start of your turn. You only have one roll this turn, but double the result.*

The highest ceiling single-turn card in the kit. Six dice become effectively twelve. Play it on a turn where the first roll is already strong. A mediocre roll doubled is still mediocre.

### Electro-Scratch *(Temporary)*
*All other monsters lose 1 HEART.*

A board-wide ping that hits everyone simultaneously. Use it to finish off a weakened monster, pressure the table, or soften up a Tokyo occupant before a claw turn.

### Mega Purr *(Temporary)*
*All other monsters give you 1 ENERGY or 1 STAR (they choose).*

Mandatory tribute from the entire table. Every opponent must hand over either energy or a VP with no option to give nothing. Best played when opponents are energy-rich and star-rich so there is no easy choice.

### Nine Lives *(Temporary)*
*If you reach 0 HEART, discard all your cards including evolutions, lose all energy and stars, and leave Tokyo. Gain 9 HEART and 9 STAR, and continue playing.*

The most dramatic evolution card in the game. Instead of being eliminated, reset to 9 health and 9 VP instantly. The cost is severe, every card and resource gone, but from 9 VP two good turns can win the game. Hold it as long as possible and let opponents think they can eliminate you.

## Tips & Strategies

### Early Game
- Get **Meow Missile** and **Mouse Hunter** active together as fast as possible. A single 1 in your roll then generates a free claw and a free VP every turn.
- Keep **Nine Lives** in hand from the moment you draw it. Its value comes entirely from being a surprise.

### Late Game
- Trigger **Nine Lives** at exactly 0 health, ideally when opponents are about to pile on. The instant 9 VP reset can flip the entire game.
- **Cat Nip** is the best closer when health is stable. Doubling a strong natural roll can end the game in a single turn.

### Evolution Combos
- **Meow Missile + Mouse Hunter + Cat Nip** — double your dice with both one-triggers active for a turn that generates VP, claws, and raw dice value simultaneously.
- **Play With Your Food + Feline Motor** — steal resources from Tokyo occupants with claws, then slide into Tokyo the moment they yield.
- **Nine Lives + Electro-Scratch or Mega Purr** — after triggering Nine Lives at 9 VP, immediately play a resource grab or board ping to accelerate the closing push.

## Common Mistakes
- Rerolling ones away out of habit, both Meow Missile and Mouse Hunter need at least one 1 to trigger
- Playing **Nine Lives** when health is merely low, save it for the moment you actually hit 0
- Using **Cat Nip** on a mediocre first roll, doubling a bad roll is still a bad roll
- Ignoring **Feline Motor** once active, track Tokyo's status during other players' turns
- Using **Mega Purr** when opponents are resource-poor, it needs a full table to be worth play
