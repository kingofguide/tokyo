## Overview

Gigazaur is a nuclear-powered wrecking machine with one goal: deal damage. His playstyle revolves around maximising claw output, keeping opponents on the back foot, and turning Tokyo into a warzone nobody else wants to enter.

## Playstyle

Gigazaur is a Brawler. You win by eliminating monsters before they can build VP leads, or by hammering anyone who dares occupy Tokyo until they give it up. He rewards players who like making big, decisive swings — subtlety is not in his vocabulary.

## Evolution Cards

Gigazaur's eight evolution cards amplify his already-aggressive dice game — turning raw rolls into precision tools and punishing anyone who dares hit back.

### Tail Sweep *(Permanent)*
Once per turn, change one of your rolled dice to a 1 or 2.

A flexible utility card. Use it to complete a three-of-a-kind number combo you just missed, or fish for VP points when you're close to winning. Pairs well with Defender of Tokyo for squeezing extra value out of number dice.

### Gamma Breath *(Permanent)*
Once per turn, change one of your rolled dice to a CLAW.

Gigazaur's bread and butter evolution. Guarantees at least one claw every turn, making your damage output completely reliable. Stack this with natural claw rolls for consistently high damage turns. One of his strongest permanents.

### Heat Vision *(Permanent)*
Monsters that wound you with CLAWS lose 1 STAR.

A passive deterrent that punishes aggression against you. Opponents thinking about attacking will have to weigh losing VP — which can meaningfully slow down a Climber or Scorer. Best in a game where multiple players are attacking you.

### Defender of Tokyo *(Permanent)*
If you start your turn in Tokyo, all other monsters lose 1 STAR.

Gigazaur's most powerful evolution. Turning Tokyo occupancy into a VP drain on every opponent per turn is devastating — it pressures Climbers and Scorers while rewarding you for staying put. Combine with Tail Sweep to keep rolling numbers and VP dice while this bleeds everyone dry.

### Saurian Adaptability *(Temporary)*
Choose a die face. Take all dice showing that face and flip them all to a single face of your choice.

An incredible tempo card. If you rolled four lightning bolts but want claws, flip them all at once. Best used to convert a bad roll into a focused, high-value turn — especially when you need a burst of claws or a big three-of-a-kind.

### Primal Bellow *(Temporary)*
All other monsters lose 2 STAR.

Two free VP off every opponent at once. In a four-player game that's 6 total VP swung in your favour in a single card. Save it for when multiple opponents are close to 20 VP, or use it as a closer when you're leading and want to stretch the gap.

### Radioactive Waste *(Temporary)*
Gain 2 ENERGY and 1 HEART.

Gigazaur doesn't usually care about energy, but this freebie lets you pick up a card you'd otherwise ignore — something like Nova Breath or Extra Head that amplifies your damage further. The bonus heart is a small but welcome top-up.

### Detachable Tail *(Temporary)*
You cannot lose HEART this turn.

A get-out-of-jail card. Use it on a turn where you're staying in Tokyo and expect heavy incoming damage, or hold it as insurance when you're low on health and can't afford to yield. Pairs beautifully with Defender of Tokyo — stay in Tokyo, drain opponents' VP, take zero damage.

## Tips & Strategies

### Early Game
- Roll as many **claws** as possible in the first two rounds — establish board presence immediately.
- Don't bother healing unless you're critically low; health is a resource to spend, not hoard.
- Getting **Gamma Breath** early essentially gives you a free claw every turn — prioritise it.

### Mid Game
- Target the monster with the most VP, not the lowest health. VP leaders are the real threat.
- Use your damage output to force expensive heals out of opponents, draining their reroll dice.
- Once **Defender of Tokyo** is active, commit to staying in Tokyo as long as possible — the passive VP drain is worth absorbing hits.

### Late Game
- If you can eliminate a monster, commit to it fully — partial damage just lets them heal.
- Switch to VP dice (3-of-a-kind) if you're already ahead; don't attack when you're winning.
- **Primal Bellow** and **Detachable Tail** are ideal closers — save them for the final push.

### Best Evolution Combos
- **Gamma Breath + Tail Sweep** — guaranteed claw plus a flexible number conversion every turn. Consistent, dominant dice control.
- **Defender of Tokyo + Detachable Tail** — stay in Tokyo for free, drain everyone's VP, take zero damage that turn.
- **Saurian Adaptability + Defender of Tokyo** — convert a bad roll into a full VP dice turn while your Tokyo passive bleeds opponents.

### Card Priorities (Base Market)
1. **Nova Breath** — hits everyone outside Tokyo, synergises perfectly with your aggression
2. **Extra Head** — doubles your claw output
3. **Complete Destruction** — nuke the board when it counts

## Common Mistakes

- Spreading damage across multiple targets instead of focusing one down
- Ignoring your own VP entirely and letting a sneaky camper hit 20
- Over-committing to fights when a VP race is faster
- Sitting on **Detachable Tail** too long — use it proactively when you need to stay in Tokyo, don't save it forever
- Underestimating **Defender of Tokyo** — players often treat Gigazaur as a pure damage dealer and miss how powerful the VP drain is
