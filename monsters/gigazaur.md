## Overview
Gigazaur is a nuclear-powered wrecking machine with one goal: deal damage. The playstyle revolves around maximising claw output, keeping opponents on the back foot, and turning Tokyo into a warzone nobody else wants to enter.

As a **Beginner** monster with a **Smasher**, **Scorer**, and **Climber** playstyle, Gigazaur is straightforward to pick up and rewarding to master.

## Playstyle
Gigazaur is a **Brawler**. The evolution cards make this clear: **Gamma Breath** guarantees a free claw every turn, **Defender of Tokyo** drains every opponent's VP just for staying in Tokyo, and **Heat Vision** punishes anyone who dares hit back. Subtlety is not part of the gameplan.

The core loop is: **roll claws, deal damage, occupy Tokyo, drain VP**.

## Evolution Cards
Gigazaur's eight evolution cards turn raw dice rolls into precision tools and punish opponents for engaging.

### Tail Sweep *(Permanent)*
*Once per turn, change one of your rolled dice to a 1 or 2.*

A flexible utility card. Use it to complete a three-of-a-kind number combo you just missed, or fish for VP points when close to winning. Pairs well with Defender of Tokyo for squeezing extra value out of number dice.

### Gamma Breath *(Permanent)*
*Once per turn, change one of your rolled dice to a CLAW.*

Gigazaur's most important evolution. Guarantees at least one claw every turn making damage output completely reliable. Stack with natural claw rolls for consistently high damage turns.

### Heat Vision *(Permanent)*
*Monsters that wound you with CLAWS lose 1 STAR.*

A passive deterrent that punishes aggression. Opponents thinking about attacking will have to weigh losing VP, which can meaningfully slow down anyone racing for points.

### Defender of Tokyo *(Permanent)*
*If you start your turn in Tokyo, all other monsters lose 1 STAR.*

Gigazaur's most powerful evolution. Turning Tokyo occupancy into a VP drain every turn is devastating. Combine with Tail Sweep to keep rolling number dice while this bleeds everyone dry.

### Saurian Adaptability *(Temporary)*
*Choose a die face. Take all dice showing that face and flip them all to a single face of your choice.*

An incredible tempo card. Convert a bad roll into a focused high-value turn instantly — flip a pile of lightning bolts into claws or a clean three-of-a-kind.

### Primal Bellow *(Temporary)*
*All other monsters lose 2 STAR.*

Strips VP from every opponent at once. Save it for when multiple opponents are close to 20 VP, or use it as a closer to stretch the gap when already leading.

### Radioactive Waste *(Temporary)*
*Gain 2 ENERGY and 1 HEART.*

Gigazaur doesn't usually care about energy, but this freebie opens up cards like Nova Breath or Extra Head. The bonus heart is a small but welcome top-up.

### Detachable Tail *(Temporary)*
*You cannot lose HEART this turn.*

A get-out-of-jail card. Use it on a turn where heavy incoming damage is expected, or hold it as insurance when health is low. Pairs perfectly with Defender of Tokyo — stay in Tokyo, drain VP, take zero damage.

## Tips & Strategies

### Early Game
- Roll as many **claws** as possible in the first two rounds. Health is a resource to spend, not hoard.
- Get **Gamma Breath** active as fast as possible. A free claw every turn makes damage output completely consistent.

### Late Game
- Commit fully to eliminating a wounded monster. Partial damage just lets them heal.
- Hold **Primal Bellow** and **Detachable Tail** for the final push.

### Evolution Combos
- **Gamma Breath + Tail Sweep** — guaranteed claw plus flexible number conversion every turn. Consistent dice control.
- **Defender of Tokyo + Detachable Tail** — stay in Tokyo, drain VP, take zero damage that turn.
- **Saurian Adaptability + Defender of Tokyo** — convert a bad roll into a VP dice turn while the Tokyo passive bleeds opponents.

## Common Mistakes
- Spreading damage across multiple targets instead of focusing one down
- Ignoring VP entirely and letting a camper quietly hit 20
- Sitting on **Detachable Tail** too long, use it proactively when staying in Tokyo
- Underestimating **Defender of Tokyo**, the VP drain is often more powerful than the damage output
