## Overview
Meka Dragon is the card-game player's monster. While others are rolling dice and punching things, energy is accumulating and converting into powerful combos that snowball out of control. The ceiling is much higher than it first appears, and the evolution cards turn the whole kit into something genuinely terrifying once they stack up.

As an **Intermediate** monster with a **Smasher** playstyle, Meka Dragon rewards patience and board reading over brute force.

## Playstyle
Meka Dragon evolutions tell the full story: **Target Acquired** marks whoever hits you hardest and amplifies every attack you land on them, **Lightning Armor** reflects damage back at attackers on a dice roll, and **Breath of Doom** turns every Tokyo hit into board-wide collateral damage. The more opponents engage, the more dangerous things get.

The core loop is: **build energy, mark targets, amplify damage, punish aggression**.

## Evolution Cards
Meka Dragon's eight evolution cards create a web of damage amplification and retribution. The more opponents attack, the more dangerous the kit becomes.

### Target Acquired *(Permanent)*
*When a monster makes you lose HEART with CLAWS, you can give them the TARGET token. That monster loses 1 extra HEART every time you deal damage to them.*

A slow-burn punisher. Mark whoever hits hardest and every subsequent attack on them deals bonus damage. Pairs exceptionally well with Claws of Steel and Mecha Blast.

### Claws of Steel *(Permanent)*
*On your turn, if you make another monster lose at least 3 HEART, they lose 1 extra HEART.*

A passive damage amplifier that rewards going big. Rolling three or more claws already puts opponents under pressure. Stack with Target Acquired to punish anyone who's been marked.

### Lightning Armor *(Permanent)*
*When you lose HEART, roll a die for each HEART lost. For each CLAW rolled, the monster whose turn it is loses 1 HEART.*

One of the most disruptive evolutions in the game. Opponents attacking have to gamble that their claw dice won't bounce straight back at them. In Tokyo this can reflect serious damage back across the table.

### Breath of Doom *(Permanent)*
*When you make monsters in Tokyo lose at least 1 HEART, monsters outside Tokyo also lose 1 HEART (except you).*

A board-wide pressure tool. Hitting Tokyo now pings every monster not in Tokyo as collateral damage. Combined with Claws of Steel a single claw turn can damage every monster on the board simultaneously.

### Tune-Up *(Temporary)*
*Play before rolling dice. If you are not in Tokyo, skip your turn, gain 4 HEART and 2 ENERGY.*

A deliberate trade — forfeit your turn entirely to fully restock. Best used when health is dangerously low and the energy engine has stalled. Never use it while in Tokyo, it only triggers outside.

### Programmed to Destroy *(Permanent)*
*Gain 3 STAR and 2 ENERGY each time a monster's health reaches 0.*

A win condition accelerator. Each elimination pays out 3 VP and 2 energy, enough to buy another card or fuel a follow-up turn. Turns kills into a snowballing resource engine.

### Destructive Analysis *(Temporary)*
*Gain 1 ENERGY for each CLAW you rolled this turn.*

The bridge between aggression and the energy engine. A turn with four claws suddenly also generates four energy. Best used after dealing damage to immediately convert momentum into resources.

### Mecha Blast *(Temporary)*
*Each monster you make lose HEART with your CLAWS loses 2 extra HEART.*

A massive burst damage card. Every monster hit this turn takes 2 bonus damage. Stack with Target Acquired and Claws of Steel for a single turn that can cripple multiple opponents. Save it for when targets are already weakened.

## Tips & Strategies

### Early Game
- Roll **lightning bolts** almost exclusively for the first few turns. The whole kit runs on energy.
- Get **Target Acquired** active early and mark the most aggressive player immediately.

### Late Game
- **Programmed to Destroy** makes finishing off weakened monsters a priority. Each kill pays 3 VP and 2 energy.
- Hold **Mecha Blast** for a turn where multiple opponents are low. The 2 bonus damage per target is devastating at that point.

### Evolution Combos
- **Target Acquired + Claws of Steel + Mecha Blast** — mark a target, guarantee the threshold bonus, then play Mecha Blast for a turn that deals massive bonus damage to the marked target.
- **Breath of Doom + Claws of Steel** — hit Tokyo hard enough to trigger the threshold and every outside monster takes 1 damage too.
- **Lightning Armor + Programmed to Destroy** — sit in Tokyo, reflect damage back, collect 3 VP and 2 energy per kill.
- **Destructive Analysis + any high-claw turn** — convert aggression directly into energy then spend it immediately.

## Common Mistakes
- Buying expensive cards before the energy engine is established
- Entering Tokyo too early, getting hit in setup turns stalls the engine before it starts
- Forgetting to actually win, energy and evolutions are a means not an end
- Wasting **Mecha Blast** on a single target, it shines when hitting multiple monsters at once
- Not marking a target with **Target Acquired** early enough, every hit taken without marking is a missed opportunity
- Using **Tune-Up** while in Tokyo, it only works outside
