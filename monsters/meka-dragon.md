## Overview

Meka Dragon is the card-game player's monster. While others are rolling dice and punching things, she's accumulating energy and converting it into powerful combos that snowball out of control. She has a lower floor than the tanks but a much higher ceiling in experienced hands — and her evolution cards turn her into something genuinely terrifying once they stack up.

## Playstyle

Meka Dragon is an **Engine Builder**. Your dice rolls should almost always prioritise **lightning bolts** over everything else in the first few rounds. Once you have 5–8 energy, you can buy powerful cards that let you outpace everyone else's raw dice rolls.

This playstyle requires reading the card market carefully and being patient early. Her evolutions reward aggression in a very specific way — she doesn't just deal damage, she punishes monsters for daring to hit back.

## Evolution Cards

Meka Dragon's eight evolution cards create a web of damage amplification and retribution. The more opponents attack her, the more dangerous she becomes.

### Target Acquired *(Permanent)*
When a monster makes you lose HEART with CLAWS, you can give them the TARGET token. That monster loses 1 extra HEART every time you deal damage to them.

A slow-burn punisher. Mark whoever hits you hardest and every subsequent attack you land on them deals bonus damage. Pairs exceptionally well with Claws of Steel and Mecha Blast — the TARGET token turns any damage you deal into something much more threatening.

### Claws of Steel *(Permanent)*
On your turn, if you make another monster lose at least 3 HEART, they lose 1 extra HEART.

A passive damage amplifier that rewards going big. Rolling three or more claws already puts opponents under pressure — this makes every strong claw turn deal 4+ damage instead of 3. Stack it with Target Acquired for a monster that punishes anyone you've marked.

### Lightning Armor *(Permanent)*
When you lose HEART, roll a die for each HEART lost. For each CLAW rolled, the monster whose turn it is loses 1 HEART.

Meka Dragon's most defensive evolution — and one of the most disruptive in the game. Opponents attacking you have to gamble that their claw dice won't bounce straight back at them. In Tokyo, where you're absorbing multiple hits, this can reflect serious damage back across the table.

### Breath of Doom *(Permanent)*
When you make monsters in Tokyo lose at least 1 HEART, monsters outside Tokyo also lose 1 HEART (except you).

A board-wide pressure tool. Hitting Tokyo now pings every monster not in Tokyo as collateral damage. Combined with Claws of Steel this can mean a single claw turn damages every monster on the board simultaneously. Devastating in a four-player game.

### Tune-Up *(Temporary)*
Play before rolling dice. If you are not in Tokyo, skip your turn, gain 4 HEART and 2 ENERGY.

A deliberate trade — forfeit your turn entirely to fully restock. Best used when your health is dangerously low and your energy engine has stalled. The 2 energy ensures you can still buy something meaningful next turn. Never use it when you're in Tokyo — you need to be outside for it to trigger.

### Programmed to Destroy *(Permanent)*
Gain 3 STAR and 2 ENERGY each time a monster's health reaches 0.

Meka Dragon's win condition accelerator. Each elimination hands her 3 VP and 2 energy — enough to buy another card or fuel a follow-up turn. In a game where you're already pressuring opponents with Target Acquired and Claws of Steel, this turns kills into a snowballing resource engine.

### Destructive Analysis *(Temporary)*
Gain 1 ENERGY for each CLAW you rolled this turn.

The bridge between aggression and her energy engine. A turn where you rolled four claws suddenly also generates four energy — enough to buy a significant card immediately. Best used on a high-claw turn where you've already dealt your damage and want to convert the momentum into resources.

### Mecha Blast *(Temporary)*
Each monster you make lose HEART with your CLAWS loses 2 extra HEART.

A massive burst damage card. Every monster you hit this turn takes 2 bonus damage — stack it with Target Acquired's bonus and Claws of Steel's threshold for a single turn that can cripple multiple monsters simultaneously. Save it for a decisive swing when opponents are already weakened.

## Tips & Strategies

### Early Game
- Roll **lightning bolts** almost exclusively for the first 2–3 turns to build your engine.
- Only enter Tokyo if it's handed to you — getting hit early disrupts your setup.
- Getting **Target Acquired** early sets a threatening tone; mark the most aggressive player immediately.

### Mid Game
- By turn 4–5 you should have cards and evolutions stacking. Start chaining them.
- **Lightning Armor** turns you into a dangerous target to attack — opponents will think twice.
- Once **Breath of Doom** is active, every Tokyo hit becomes board-wide — prioritise claw dice when you have it.

### Late Game
- A fully built Meka Dragon can win in a single round with the right combo.
- **Programmed to Destroy** makes finishing off weakened monsters a priority — each kill pays dividends.
- Hold **Mecha Blast** for a turn where multiple opponents are low — the 2 bonus damage per target is devastating.

### Best Evolution Combos
- **Target Acquired + Claws of Steel + Mecha Blast** — mark a target, guarantee the threshold bonus, then play Mecha Blast for a turn that deals 3+ bonus damage to your marked target alone.
- **Breath of Doom + Claws of Steel** — hit Tokyo hard enough to trigger the threshold, and every monster outside Tokyo takes 1 damage too. A single turn pings the whole board.
- **Lightning Armor + Programmed to Destroy** — sit in Tokyo, reflect damage back, finish off whoever gets low enough and collect 3 VP and 2 energy per kill.
- **Destructive Analysis + any high-claw turn** — convert aggression directly into energy, then immediately spend it on a card. Keeps both engines running simultaneously.

### Card Priorities (Base Market)
1. **Apartment Building** — massive VP burst, pairs with your energy advantage
2. **Nova Breath** — hits all outside-Tokyo monsters, stacks with Breath of Doom
3. **Acid Attack** — persistent damage that compounds over time
4. **Eater of the Dead** — win condition in attrition games, combos with Programmed to Destroy

## Common Mistakes

- Buying expensive cards before your energy engine is established
- Entering Tokyo too early — getting hit in setup turns stalls your engine before it starts
- Forgetting to actually *win* — energy and evolutions are a means, not an end
- Wasting **Mecha Blast** on a single target — it shines when you can hit multiple monsters at once
- Not marking a target with **Target Acquired** early enough — every hit you take without marking someone is a missed opportunity
- Using **Tune-Up** while in Tokyo — it only works outside, and staying in Tokyo while low on health is often the wrong call anyway
