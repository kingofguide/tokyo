## Overview

Space Penguin is the most disruptive monster in the game. He doesn't win by dealing the most damage or sitting in Tokyo the longest — he wins by dismantling everyone else's strategy while quietly building his own. He copies evolution cards, locks dice, strips rerolls, and can freeze the entire board mid-game. In the right hands he's infuriating to play against and deeply satisfying to pilot.

He's rated Advanced because his value comes almost entirely from reading what opponents are doing and reacting to it. If you don't know what other monsters' evolutions do, Space Penguin loses most of his teeth.

## Playstyle

Space Penguin is a **Disruptor**. He doesn't have a single fixed gameplan — his strategy reshapes itself around whatever the table is doing. His evolutions punish aggression, slow down fast decks, copy the best tools on the board, and deny opponents the rerolls they depend on.

His core loop is: **observe what's working at the table → copy or counter it → buy time while opponents stall → close out with VP**.

He rewards players who pay close attention to other monsters' boards and know when to disrupt versus when to accelerate their own game.

## Evolution Cards

Space Penguin's eight evolution cards are among the most unique in the game — several of them interact directly with other players' cards and dice in ways no other monster can.

### Icy Reflection *(Permanent)*
Choose an evolution card in front of another monster and place a Snowflake token on it. Icy Reflection becomes a copy of that card. If the copied card is removed from play, Icy Reflection is discarded.

Space Penguin's most defining card. You don't build your own engine — you steal someone else's. Copy Gigazaur's Gamma Breath for free claw manipulation, grab Meka Dragon's Lightning Armor to reflect damage, or clone The King's Twas Beauty Killed the Beast for instant VP generation. The card is only as strong as what's available to copy, which is why knowing other monsters' evolutions is essential. Watch out for the linked discard — if the original card leaves play, yours goes with it.

### Black Diamond *(Permanent)*
Gain 1 extra STAR each time you take control of Tokyo or choose to stay in Tokyo when you could have yielded.

A VP engine that rewards decisiveness. Every time you actively choose to hold Tokyo under fire you bank a bonus star on top of the standard reward. It doesn't require staying long — even a series of quick in-and-out Tokyo grabs generates consistent VP. Pairs well with **Cold Wave** and **Blizzard** since limiting opponent dice makes staying in Tokyo much safer.

### Freeze Ray *(Permanent)*
When you wound a monster in Tokyo, give them this card. At the start of their turn they choose a die face — that face has no effect this turn. You take the card back at the end of their turn.

A recurring debuff that bounces between you and Tokyo's occupant. Each time you hit the Tokyo monster, they lose a die face for their next turn — no claws, no lightning bolts, no hearts, whatever they most need gone. It doesn't cost you anything to keep triggering it as long as you keep dealing damage. Particularly strong against energy-dependent monsters who rely on lightning bolt turns.

### Encased in Ice *(Permanent)*
Spend 1 ENERGY to choose one of your rolled dice. That die is frozen until the start of your next turn — it cannot be changed and is used normally by other monsters during the Resolve Dice phase.

Dice manipulation with a twist. Lock in a claw, a lightning bolt, or a number die before rerolling the rest — guaranteeing at least one result. The frozen die also interacts with other players' Resolve Dice effects, which can be used tactically to interfere with cards that trigger off specific faces. Costs energy but pays off consistently as a precision tool.

### Miraculous Catch *(Permanent)*
Once per turn during the Buy Power Cards phase, shuffle the discard pile and reveal one card randomly. Buy it for 1 ENERGY less than normal or discard it. The rest of the discard pile goes back.

Access to cards everyone else already passed on — at a discount. The randomness is real but the energy reduction means you can snap up expensive cards that would otherwise be out of reach. Best used when the face-up market is weak or when you know a powerful card was recently discarded. Stacks well with Space Penguin's energy generation to keep buying every turn.

### Deep Dive *(Temporary)*
Look at the top 3 Power cards of the deck. Choose one and play it in front of you for free. Put the others on the bottom.

A free card from the top of the deck with full information. Unlike Miraculous Catch this is deterministic — you see three options and pick the best one. Playing a keep card for free is always strong. Use it when the face-up market has nothing you want or when you need a specific type of card to complete a combo.

### Cold Wave *(Temporary)*
Until your next turn, all other monsters roll with 1 fewer die.

Board-wide disruption on a timer. Every opponent loses a die for a full round — fewer claws, fewer lightning bolts, fewer VP numbers. Best played on your turn just before a round where multiple opponents are likely to take aggressive or high-value turns. Combine with **Blizzard** in the same round for total dice lockdown.

### Blizzard *(Temporary)*
Play during your turn. Until the start of your next turn, monsters only have a single roll and cannot yield Tokyo.

Space Penguin's most powerful disruption card. Two effects at once: strip all rerolls from every opponent for a full round, and lock the current Tokyo occupant in place — they cannot yield no matter how much damage they take. Use it when a monster in Tokyo is low on health to guarantee they take everything coming at them, or when multiple opponents are sitting on powerful dice combinations they were planning to reroll into.

## Tips & Strategies

### Early Game
- Scout other monsters' evolutions immediately — **Icy Reflection** is your most valuable card and its ceiling depends entirely on what's available to copy.
- Roll lightning bolts early. Your kit is energy-hungry and you need reserves for **Encased in Ice** and **Miraculous Catch**.
- **Freeze Ray** is most effective against high-energy monsters early — crippling their lightning bolt turns before their engine starts is much easier than countering it mid-game.

### Mid Game
- **Icy Reflection** targets should shift as the game evolves — copy whatever is currently the strongest active evolution on the board.
- Use **Cold Wave** or **Blizzard** when opponents are about to take a turn that could end the game or significantly change the board state.
- **Black Diamond** rewards opportunistic Tokyo grabs — you don't need to camp, just dip in and out decisively.

### Late Game
- **Blizzard** is your best closer — locking rerolls and forcing Tokyo hold means a weak occupant can't escape punishment.
- If you're near 20 VP, **Deep Dive** can find the card that gets you there immediately.
- Watch for **Miraculous Catch** value late when the discard pile is full of expensive cards other players passed on.

### Best Evolution Combos
- **Icy Reflection + any strong permanent** — copying The King's Twas Beauty Killed the Beast or Gigazaur's Defender of Tokyo gives you VP generation you never had to earn.
- **Cold Wave + Blizzard** — play both in the same round for a full dice lockdown. Opponents get one roll with one fewer die and no rerolls. Devastating during a critical round.
- **Freeze Ray + Encased in Ice** — debuff the Tokyo occupant's dice while locking in your best result before rerolling. Controls both sides of the roll simultaneously.
- **Black Diamond + Blizzard** — force the Tokyo occupant to stay put, then grab Tokyo yourself on a subsequent turn for the bonus VP.

### Card Priorities (Base Market)
1. **Freeze Time** — stalling opponents while your disruption compounds is extremely powerful
2. **Apartment Building** — converts your energy advantage into VP efficiently
3. **Herd Culler** — cheap damage that keeps opponents' health low for Blizzard finishes
4. **Background Dweller** — staying out of Tokyo safely while accumulating energy suits Space Penguin's patient gameplan

## Common Mistakes

- Using **Icy Reflection** on a weak evolution just because it's available — wait for something genuinely powerful to copy
- Playing **Blizzard** too early before multiple opponents are vulnerable — it's a one-shot, make it count
- Ignoring energy generation — **Encased in Ice** and **Miraculous Catch** both cost energy and run dry fast without lightning bolt investment
- Forgetting that **Freeze Ray** bounces back to you — don't lose track of it or you lose the recurring debuff
- Treating Space Penguin like a damage dealer — his aggression stats are low for a reason, VP comes from disruption and card value, not claws
- Using **Cold Wave** and **Blizzard** on separate turns when stacking them in the same round is dramatically more effective
