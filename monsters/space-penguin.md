## Overview
Space Penguin is the most disruptive monster in the game. Winning comes not from dealing the most damage or sitting in Tokyo the longest, but from dismantling everyone else's strategy while quietly building an advantage. Evolutions copy other monsters' cards, lock dice, strip rerolls, and can freeze the entire board mid-game.

Rated **Advanced** with a **Disruptor** and **Collector** playstyle, Space Penguin rewards players who pay close attention to other monsters' boards. If you don't know what other evolutions do, most of the kit loses its teeth.

## Playstyle
Space Penguin is a **Disruptor** with no fixed gameplan. The evolutions define this completely: **Icy Reflection** copies whatever the strongest evolution on the board is, **Blizzard** strips all rerolls and locks the Tokyo occupant in place, and **Cold Wave** costs every opponent a die for a full round. The strategy reshapes itself around whatever the table is doing.

The core loop is: **observe what's working, copy or counter it, buy time while opponents stall, close out with VP**.

## Evolution Cards
Space Penguin's eight evolution cards are among the most unique in the game. Several interact directly with other players' cards and dice in ways no other monster can.

### Icy Reflection *(Permanent)*
*Choose an evolution card in front of another monster and place a Snowflake token on it. Icy Reflection becomes a copy of that card. If the copied card is removed from play, Icy Reflection is discarded.*

The most defining card in the kit. Instead of building an engine, steal someone else's. The card is only as strong as what's available to copy, which is why knowing other monsters' evolutions is essential. Watch the linked discard — if the original card leaves play, so does this one.

### Black Diamond *(Permanent)*
*Gain 1 extra STAR each time you take control of Tokyo or choose to stay when you could have yielded.*

A VP engine that rewards decisiveness. Every active Tokyo hold banks a bonus star. Doesn't require camping — quick in-and-out grabs generate consistent VP. Pairs well with Cold Wave and Blizzard since limiting opponent dice makes staying in Tokyo much safer.

### Freeze Ray *(Permanent)*
*When you wound a monster in Tokyo, give them this card. At the start of their turn they choose a die face — that face has no effect this turn. You take the card back at the end of their turn.*

A recurring debuff that bounces between you and the Tokyo occupant. Each time you hit the Tokyo monster, they lose a die face for their next turn. Particularly strong against energy-dependent monsters who rely on lightning bolt turns.

### Encased in Ice *(Permanent)*
*Spend 1 ENERGY to choose one of your rolled dice. That die is frozen until the start of your next turn — it cannot be changed and is used normally by other monsters during the Resolve Dice phase.*

Lock in a claw, a lightning bolt, or a number die before rerolling the rest, guaranteeing at least one result. Costs energy but pays off consistently as a precision tool.

### Miraculous Catch *(Permanent)*
*Once per turn during the Buy Power Cards phase, shuffle the discard pile and reveal one card randomly. Buy it for 1 ENERGY less than normal or discard it.*

Access to cards everyone else already passed on, at a discount. Best used when the face-up market is weak or when a powerful card was recently discarded. Stacks well with energy generation to keep buying every turn.

### Deep Dive *(Temporary)*
*Look at the top 3 Power cards of the deck. Choose one and play it in front of you for free. Put the others on the bottom.*

A free card with full information. Unlike Miraculous Catch this is deterministic. Use it when the face-up market has nothing useful or when a specific card type is needed to complete a combo.

### Cold Wave *(Temporary)*
*Until your next turn, all other monsters roll with 1 fewer die.*

Board-wide disruption on a timer. Every opponent loses a die for a full round. Best played just before a round where opponents are likely to take aggressive or high-value turns. Stack with Blizzard in the same round for total dice lockdown.

### Blizzard *(Temporary)*
*Play during your turn. Until the start of your next turn, monsters only have a single roll and cannot yield Tokyo.*

The most powerful disruption card in the kit. Strips all rerolls from every opponent for a full round and locks the Tokyo occupant in place. Use it when a Tokyo occupant is low on health, or when opponents are sitting on powerful dice combinations they were planning to reroll into.

## Tips & Strategies

### Early Game
- Scout other monsters' evolutions immediately. **Icy Reflection** is the most valuable card and its ceiling depends entirely on what's available to copy.
- Roll lightning bolts early. The kit is energy-hungry and reserves are needed for **Encased in Ice** and **Miraculous Catch**.

### Late Game
- **Blizzard** is the best closer. Locking rerolls and forcing a Tokyo hold means a weak occupant can't escape punishment.
- Watch for **Miraculous Catch** value when the discard pile is full of expensive cards others passed on.

### Evolution Combos
- **Icy Reflection + any strong permanent** — copying a top-tier evolution from another monster gives VP generation or damage output that was never earned independently.
- **Cold Wave + Blizzard** — play both in the same round for a full dice lockdown. Opponents get one roll with one fewer die and no rerolls.
- **Freeze Ray + Encased in Ice** — debuff the Tokyo occupant's dice while locking in your best result before rerolling.
- **Black Diamond + Blizzard** — force the Tokyo occupant to stay put, then grab Tokyo for the bonus VP.

## Common Mistakes
- Using **Icy Reflection** on a weak evolution just because it's available, wait for something genuinely powerful
- Playing **Blizzard** too early before multiple opponents are vulnerable, it's a one-shot
- Ignoring energy generation, both **Encased in Ice** and **Miraculous Catch** run dry fast without lightning bolt investment
- Forgetting that **Freeze Ray** bounces back, losing track of it means losing the recurring debuff
- Treating Space Penguin like a damage dealer, VP comes from disruption and card value not claws
- Using **Cold Wave** and **Blizzard** on separate turns when stacking them in the same round is far more effective
