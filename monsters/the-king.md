## Overview

The King is a giant gorilla with one obsession: owning Tokyo. Every evolution card he has either rewards him for being in Tokyo, punishes opponents for trying to remove him, or gets him back in the moment he's forced out. He's not interested in energy or card engines — he wants the throne, and he wants it now.

He's a strong pick for players who enjoy positional play and VP accumulation over raw aggression.

## Playstyle

The King is a **Defender**. His entire kit is built around controlling Tokyo and converting that control into victory points faster than any other monster. Where other monsters treat Tokyo as a tactical tool, The King treats it as a permanent residence.

His core loop is: **get into Tokyo → stay there → stack VP from multiple sources simultaneously → never let anyone take it**.

The twist is that his evolutions also give him tools to reclaim Tokyo instantly if he's ever forced out — making him uniquely slippery for a monster whose whole identity is standing still.

## Evolution Cards

The King's eight evolution cards are almost entirely focused on Tokyo control and VP generation. Getting even two or three of them active simultaneously creates a VP engine that's very hard to race against.

### Twas Beauty Killed the Beast *(Permanent)*
While in Tokyo, gain 1 STAR at the end of every monster's turn including yours. Discard this card and lose all your STAR if you leave Tokyo.

The King's most powerful and most dangerous evolution. In a four-player game, this generates 4 VP per full round — compounding fast. The catch is brutal: leaving Tokyo costs you every star you've accumulated on this card. Never play it unless you're committed to staying put, have enough health to absorb pressure, and have Simian Scamper or Chest Thumping ready as insurance.

### I Am the King! *(Permanent)*
Gain 1 extra STAR when you take control of Tokyo or start your turn there.

A straightforward VP amplifier that stacks with the base game's existing Tokyo rewards. Taking Tokyo now gives 2 VP instead of 1, and starting your turn there gives 2 VP instead of 1. Low risk, permanent benefit — get this out early and it pays off every single turn.

### Alpha Male *(Permanent)*
If you wound at least one monster with CLAWS, gain 1 STAR.

Turns every claw turn into a VP gain regardless of whether you're in Tokyo. Makes The King a legitimate VP threat even on turns he's outside Tokyo — and means opponents can't ignore him just because he yielded. Stack with I Am the King! for a monster generating VP from multiple sources every turn.

### Chest Thumping *(Permanent)*
You can force monsters you wound with CLAWS to yield Tokyo.

Control made absolute. Instead of waiting for an opponent to choose to yield, you decide. Hit a Tokyo occupant hard enough and you can eject them immediately and claim the throne — no negotiation. Pairs perfectly with Twas Beauty Killed the Beast, since getting back into Tokyo on demand protects your VP stack.

### Giant Banana *(Temporary)*
Gain 2 HEART.

Simple sustain. Use it when your health drops low enough that staying in Tokyo becomes risky. Two health can be the difference between riding out one more round of attacks and being forced to yield and losing everything on Twas Beauty Killed the Beast.

### Jungle Frenzy *(Temporary)*
Play at the end of your movement phase, if you wounded the Tokyo controller with CLAWS but didn't take control. Take an extra turn.

A precision card that rewards aggressive outside play. If you hit the Tokyo occupant hard enough to wound them but they didn't yield, you get a full bonus turn immediately. Use it to keep pressing — attack again, roll for VP, or set up the killing blow. One of the strongest tempo cards in The King's kit.

### Simian Scamper *(Temporary)*
If you yield Tokyo, do not lose HEART. You can't lose HEART this turn.

The safety net for Twas Beauty Killed the Beast. If you're forced to yield and your accumulated stars would be lost, at least you walk away at full health. Also useful as a general defensive card when opponents are stacking up claw damage and you need to survive the round.

### Monkey Rush *(Temporary)*
Play when the monster controlling Tokyo leaves or is eliminated. Take control of Tokyo.

An instant Tokyo grab triggered by someone else's misfortune. Hold this in hand and you're always one monster's bad luck away from reclaiming the throne. Devastatingly good when played in response to a high-health monster being forced out — you slide in before anyone else can react.

## Tips & Strategies

### Early Game
- Get into Tokyo as fast as possible and establish **I Am the King!** first — it's low risk and pays off every turn from the moment it hits.
- Hold **Monkey Rush** in hand at all times until you need Tokyo back urgently.
- Roll claws early to pressure the current Tokyo occupant and set up **Chest Thumping**.

### Mid Game
- Once you're established in Tokyo, play **Twas Beauty Killed the Beast** — but only when you have enough health to commit.
- Keep **Giant Banana** or **Simian Scamper** as insurance before activating Twas Beauty Killed the Beast.
- Use **Chest Thumping** to control who occupies Tokyo — eject whoever poses the biggest threat.

### Late Game
- With **Twas Beauty Killed the Beast** and **I Am the King!** both active, you're generating 5+ VP per round passively. Play defensively and let the VP clock win for you.
- **Jungle Frenzy** is a strong closer — a bonus turn when you're near 20 VP can end the game immediately.
- Watch opponents' energy stockpiles carefully. A player who can one-shot your health and force a yield is your biggest threat.

### Best Evolution Combos
- **Twas Beauty Killed the Beast + I Am the King! + Alpha Male** — VP from three sources every round. Each turn in Tokyo generates 2 VP from I Am the King!, up to 4 VP from Twas Beauty, and 1 VP from any claw turn. Opponents must deal with you immediately or lose the VP race.
- **Chest Thumping + Monkey Rush** — force anyone out of Tokyo with claws, then instantly reclaim it with Monkey Rush if you ever lose it. Near-permanent Tokyo control.
- **Twas Beauty Killed the Beast + Simian Scamper** — stay in Tokyo accumulating VP safely, with Simian Scamper as the escape hatch that doesn't cost you health when you finally have to yield.

### Card Priorities (Base Market)
1. **Regeneration** — keeps you in Tokyo longer, protecting your VP stack
2. **Even Bigger** — extra health extends your Tokyo stays significantly
3. **Armor Plating** — reduces incoming damage, making you harder to force out
4. **Domination** — if you're already winning the VP race, extra VP from Tokyo turns closes it faster

## Common Mistakes

- Activating **Twas Beauty Killed the Beast** too early before you have health insurance — losing all your accumulated stars to a forced yield is catastrophic
- Holding **Monkey Rush** too long and letting someone else grab Tokyo when you needed it
- Ignoring **Alpha Male** — VP from claw turns outside Tokyo is often overlooked and adds up faster than expected
- Staying in Tokyo at 3-4 health hoping to survive one more round — know when to yield intentionally before being forced out
- Forgetting that **Chest Thumping** is proactive, not reactive — use it to eject threats before they become dangerous, not after
